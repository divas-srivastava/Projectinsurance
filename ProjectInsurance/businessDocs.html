Document required

    The documents required for a business loan can vary by lender, but typically include:
  • Business Plan: A detailed plan outlining your business model, goals, and how you intend to use the loan.
  • Financial Statements: This usually includes:
    • Profit and loss statements
    • Balance sheets
    • Cash flow statements
  • Tax Returns: Personal and business tax returns for the past 1-3 years.
  • Bank Statements: Recent bank statements for your business account, often for the last 3-6 months.
  • Business Licenses and Permits: Proof that your business is legally registered and compliant with local regulations.
  • Credit History: A personal and/or business credit report may be requested.
  • Identification: Personal identification, such as a driver's license or passport, for the business owner(s).
  • Collateral Documentation: If you're providing collateral, documentation related to those assets may be needed.
  • Ownership Documents: Articles of incorporation, operating agreements, or partnership agreements if applicable.
  • Debt Schedule: A list of existing business debts, including amounts, lenders, and repayment terms.
    • Always check with your lender for their specific requirements, as they may have additional documentation requests based on your business type or loan amount.
