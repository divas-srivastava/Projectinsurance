Eligibility Criteria

    Eligibility criteria for business loans can vary by lender and type of loan, but common requirements typically include:
  • Business Age: Many lenders prefer businesses that have been operational for at least 1-2 years.
  • Credit Score: A good personal and/or business credit score is often required. Generally, a score of 650 or higher is considered favorable.
  • Business Revenue: Lenders usually look for a minimum annual revenue, which can vary significantly depending on the loan size.
  • Financial Statements: You may need to provide financial documents, such as profit and loss statements, balance sheets, and tax returns.
  • Business Plan: A solid business plan demonstrating how the loan will help grow the business can be important.
  • Collateral: Some loans may require collateral, such as assets or inventory, to secure the loan.
  • Industry Type: Certain industries may face more scrutiny or restrictions based on lender policies.
  • Ownership and Management: Lenders may evaluate the experience and background of the business owner(s).
  • Legal Compliance: The business must comply with local, state, and federal regulations.
    • Always check with specific lenders for their exact criteria, as requirements can differ widely.
